It doesn't fire alerts — it understands behavior. It doesn't chase intruders — it moves the walls.

Traditional SIEM tools search logs after the fact. Hollow Purple maintains a live, temporal graph of every identity, role, and privilege edge across AWS, GCP, and Azure — then watches how that graph moves.

When a behavior repeats with structural consistency, the Copy Layer remembers it as a pattern. When a pattern crosses a confidence threshold, MAHORAGHA proposes a mutation — never applies one — and waits for a human to pull the lever.

Three phases, one nervous system

Cloud events flow through an append-only ledger into a temporal graph, get compared against behavioral baselines, and — only after persistence and isomorphism checks — reach the adaptive defense engine.
